## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have the following:

<Callout kind="info">

- A modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Safari
- An email address for account verification
- No prior installation required – everything runs in your browser

</Callout>

mwP Сoub provides a seamless onboarding experience. You complete setup in under 5 minutes.

## Sign Up and Log In

Follow these steps to create your account and access the dashboard.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit mwP Сoub" icon="globe">
    Open your browser and navigate to `https://dashboard.example.com`. Click the **Sign Up** button in the top right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Your Details" icon="user">
    Provide your email, create a strong password, and complete the CAPTCHA. Click **Create Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from mwP Сoub. Click the link to activate your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log In" icon="log-in">
    Return to the dashboard, enter your credentials, and click **Log In**. You now see the welcome screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Enable two-factor authentication immediately after login for added security.
</Callout>

## Create Your First Documentation Project

Once logged in, set up your initial project space.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New Project" icon="plus">
    From the dashboard, click **New Documentation Space**. Enter a name like "My Project Docs".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Template" icon="layout">
    Select the **Blank Space** template for full control or **Starter Docs** for pre-built structure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize Settings" icon="settings">
    Set your brand color to `#3B82F6` and add a description: "Official docs for my project."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Publish" icon="save">
    Click **Create Space**. Your project appears in the sidebar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Organize Initial Content

Structure your space with pages and sections.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add Pages" icon="file-plus">
    In your project sidebar, click **New Page**. Name it "Introduction".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add MDX Content" icon="code">
    Paste a sample MDX block to test:

    <CodeGroup tabs="MDX,Bash">
    ````mdx
    ---
    title: Introduction
    ---

    ## Overview

    Welcome to `{projectName}` docs.
    ````

    ````bash
    # Preview your changes
    npm run dev
    ````
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish Changes" icon="upload-cloud">
    Click **Publish** to make your docs live at `https://docs.example.com/my-project`.
  </Step>
</Steps>
